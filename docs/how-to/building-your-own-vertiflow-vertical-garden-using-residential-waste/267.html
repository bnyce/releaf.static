Building Your Own VertiFlow Vertical Garden Using Residential Waste

How To
by bryceb

Vertical gardens are an innovative solution for urban gardening, but did you know you can create one using household waste? In this how-to guide, we'll show you how to build a VertiFlow vertical garden using everyday items like aluminum cans, cardboard, and wire. Not only will you be creating a beautiful garden feature, but you'll also be giving new life to items that might otherwise end up in the trash. Let's get started!

Materials:

- Aluminum cans and pet food canisters
- Cardboard, paper, or fabric
- Wire, string, or zip-ties

Step 1: Building the Synthetic Stem

Begin by puncturing the bottom of each can. A nail or a screwdriver will work well for this step. The holes should be big enough to thread your wire or string through but small enough to hold the can securely.

Next, string together the cans along a vertical axis. Make sure the wire at the bottom has a snag so the cans won't pull through. Also, ensure the top end of the wire is long enough to create a loop for hanging your VertiFlow garden later.

Step 2: Wrapping the Synthetic Stem

Once your stem of cans is assembled, wrap it with cardboard, paper, or fabric. This will create a nurturing environment for your plants to grow and also helps to retain moisture.

Use string or zip-ties to hold your wrapping material in place around the cans. Ensure it's secure but not too tight; you want to allow for some air circulation.

Step 3: Setting Up Your VertiFlow Garden

Now it's time to find a home for your VertiFlow. You can suspend it from a sturdy hook or lean it against a fence or trellis. Make sure it's in a location that gets appropriate sunlight for the plants you're intending to grow.

Step 4: Adding Plants

Water your VertiFlow from above so that the water trickles down through each can. Once it's moist, add your plant cuttings. You can use a variety of plants, but those that propagate well from cuttings, like succulents or ivy, work particularly well. Insert the cuttings into the cans, making sure they're securely tucked into the wrapping material.

Remember, not all cuttings will thrive, and that's okay. Failed cuttings contribute to the linear strength of your garden and also add nutrients as they decompose.

Step 5: Ongoing Care

As your VertiFlow garden settles in, you may need to add more wrapping material if it begins to deteriorate or if your garden needs additional support. Regular watering, adequate sunlight, and occasional feeding with compost or organic fertilizer will also keep your VertiFlow garden thriving.

Building a VertiFlow vertical garden is an excellent project for green thumbs looking to reduce waste and make the most of limited space. Happy gardening!
