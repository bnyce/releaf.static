Nuts! Austin's Bumper Crop 2040

SolarPunk Fiction
by bryceb

I remember when we thought it would be a good idea to plant pecan trees all over Austin. The vision was noble: urban tree planting for more green spaces, clean air, and free pecans. Oh, how we've succeeded. But who could have predicted that we'd turn Austin into the unofficial Pecan Capital of the World?

As I walk down the city streets, I can't help but marvel at the bountiful pecan trees lining every block. Pecans are everywhere, littering the sidewalks and filling up bags at local markets. It's turned into an annual pecan frenzy that some have lovingly dubbed "The Great Pecan Overflow".

Over the years, the humble pecan has evolved from just being a staple in pies to a star ingredient in an astonishing variety of dishes. You'd think we were in a city-wide cooking show! Chefs and home cooks alike have started crafting innovative recipes - Pecan Hummus, Pecan-crusted Tofu, Pecan Milk, even Pecan Burgers!

And it's not just the food scene that has changed. Our ever-resourceful community has started transforming pecan shells into unique, sustainable furniture. Pecan-shell tables, chairs, and even lamps are becoming the new normal in Austin homes. They’re a true testament to our city’s dedication to sustainable living and creative resource use.

Even more remarkable, however, is the emergence of annual conferences focused solely on pecan tech. Scientists, entrepreneurs, and pecan enthusiasts gather to discuss the potential of pecan biofuel, advancements in pecan farming tech, and breakthroughs in pecan waste composting.

Yet, even as we celebrate our abundance, the question remains: what do we do with all these pecans? It's become a beloved city controversy. Every year, we come together to brainstorm new uses, to devise plans, to find a solution to our delightful problem.

After all, we're Austin. We're creative, resourceful, and always up for a challenge. So bring it on, pecans. We're ready for you. We might even turn you into the next big renewable energy source. Now wouldn't that be nuts?
