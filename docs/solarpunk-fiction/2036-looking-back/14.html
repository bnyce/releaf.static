editor's note: this is fiction but we want to present a picture of a possible future, not to trivialize real suffering.

It has been a full dozen years since I left Venezuela, a country in crisis, at the tender age of 12. I journeyed to Austin, Texas - a city I'd only ever seen in pictures - where I found not just refuge, but a vibrant community and unforeseen opportunities.Â 

Among the most impactful was my introduction to ReLeaf Cooperative, an innovative organization transforming waste into vertical gardens. As a newcomer in town, I joined one of their workshops, a simple introduction into creating modular planters from upcycled materials. Little did I know then, it was much more than a workshop; it was the beginning of a life-changing journey.

I remember how carefully I assembled my first planter. I'd brought with me from Venezuela an ingrained resourcefulness born of necessity, and this became my guiding principle. Each planter was a testament to the power of transformation, echoing my own journey from upheaval to stability.

Then, something unexpected happened. My work caught the attention of a local government representative. She appreciated the thoughtfulness and quality I put into each planter. Before long, my creations were gracing the State Capitol grounds, their flourishing greenery standing tall against the Texas sky.

These planters, born of upcycled materials and careful craftsmanship, began to generate a steady income stream. This financial stability was a welcome change, but more importantly, it allowed me to give back. A portion of the income started funding scholarships for new immigrants, giving them an opportunity to learn about upcycling and sustainable living.

Today, I can't help but marvel at my journey. I arrived in Austin as a 12-year-old boy escaping crisis, and found myself part of an incredible story of resilience and growth. My planters, scattered across the State Capitol grounds, continue to support and empower new arrivals in the city, just as I was supported.

The last twelve years have been transformational, not only for me, but for Austin itself. It's a city where waste is seen as a resource and sustainability is a cherished value. As I reflect on my journey, gratitude wells up within me for ReLeaf Cooperative, a cornerstone of my story and an enduring symbol of Austin's commitment to sustainable living.
